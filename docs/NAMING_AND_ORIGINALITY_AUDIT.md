# Naming and Originality Audit

Audit date: 2026-07-18

This document records a practical collision screen for the core public-facing names and terms locked during Gate 2. It is not a legal opinion, trademark registration search, or guarantee of worldwide availability. Move names, minor prop labels, and other lower-level strings still receive a final release search and protected-reference review. Before commercial marketing or trademark filing, a qualified professional should conduct a jurisdiction- and category-specific clearance search.

## Method

The screen used exact quoted-name web searches, followed by targeted game, character, creature, product, and trademark terms when useful. A candidate was rejected when its exact spelling was already a live brand, registered/applied-for mark, named game creature, or established fictional identity. Incidental usernames, surnames, OCR noise, ordinary words, and unrelated place references were recorded but were not automatically disqualifying.

This screen supplements the clean-room rules in `docs/reference-study.md`. It does not authorize copying any result's appearance, writing, mechanics, audio, or other expression.

## Originality requirements still in force

- Names passing this screen do not prove that a visual design is original. Every concept still requires a protected-reference similarity review at art Gate 1.
- Do not use the names, silhouettes, color layouts, surface motifs, poses, move presentation, UI, story language, or audiovisual identity of Pokémon, Pandemonium, Flight Rising, or any search result.
- Concepts generated with GPT Image must use the approved art bible and roster descriptions, then receive anatomy, identity, perspective, fake-text, and clean-room review before Blender production.
- `data/ids/serialized_ids.lock` is the canonical append-only registry for every numeric named `*Id` or `*IdValue` enum and every durable save-bit domain, including the specialized spawn, cutscene, dialogue-once, examine-once, and NPC-once registries. Gate 2 approval freezes its reviewed bytes in Git history. Thereafter every committed, indexed, and working-tree evolution must be an append or a same-domain, same-index `LIVE` to `TOMBSTONE` transition; deletion, reordering, renaming, resurrection, or value reuse fails closed. A later display-name change must retain or explicitly migrate the shipped numeric ID once a public ROM has consumed it.
- Repeat exact-name and marketplace/trademark screening before public release because search results and registrations change over time.
