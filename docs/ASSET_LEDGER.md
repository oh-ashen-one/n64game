# Asset Provenance and Review Ledger

No production asset may ship without a completed ledger entry. “Original” and “AI-generated” are not sufficient provenance descriptions by themselves.

## Status vocabulary

- `planned`: inventory entry exists; production has not begun
- `concept`: concept sources exist but are not approved
- `source`: editable production source exists
- `converted`: runtime conversion succeeds
- `review`: undergoing the seven mandatory art gates
- `approved`: all gates passed with evidence

Status is not a quality score. It is valid only with the gate vector and revision rules below:

| Status | Minimum valid state |
|---|---|
| `planned` | Inventory ID/profile/tier exists; no production source or gate pass is claimed |
| `concept` | Explicit ledger row exists; Gate 1 is in progress but has not passed; creator, sources/prompts, input rights, and intended output are recorded |
| `source` | Gate 1 passed and editable source exists; Gate 2 may be in review. Before benchmark approval, a visual/audio row also needs its exact whitelist subset, populated `WB-###` authorization basis, explicit ledger row, and matching current source/output hashes. Camera tuples do not use this status until genuine Gate 1 pass |
| `converted` | Gates 1–4 passed and deterministic Gate 5 output exists with hashes; the global benchmark/toolchain gate permits it |
| `review` | Gates 1–5 passed and Gate 6 or 7 is actively under review for the same source/output revision |
| `approved` | Seven ordered `pass`/allowed-equivalent decisions, zero open critical/high/medium defects, complete provenance, immutable evidence, and required `H2` polish passes |

A later source hash invalidates downstream decisions. A later generated-output hash invalidates Gates 5–7. A benchmark-source hash change also invalidates the global approval record. Status may move backward; it may never skip the ordered evidence requirements.

A failed revision is represented by machine decision `fail` plus disposition `revise` or `rebuild` in its immutable gate/review records; `rejected` is not a separate current-ledger status because one mutable row cannot safely preserve a versioned rejection tombstone.

## Review gates

1. Concept/orthographic
2. Blender topology, normals, UV, scale, rig, and naming
3. Textured turntable
4. Animation and deformation
5. Tiny3D conversion
6. In-engine gameplay-camera, lighting, memory, and performance
7. Final consistency and polish

### Required provenance record

The wide ledger row is the audit index; complex work links the exact canonical `review/<asset_id>/g1/PROVENANCE.md`. Before `concept`, the row/record must enumerate:

- actual human/agent/tool creator roles and the project rights holder separately;
- every source path or URL, byte hash, acquisition date, purpose, input license/terms version, permission evidence, attribution/notice obligation, and clean-room restriction;
- for generated work: exact model/product/version, generation date, complete prompt and negative constraints, seed/job metadata when exposed, every selected output hash, and the governing output-rights basis;
- every material transformation and human edit in order, including paintover, anatomy repair, topology/UV/rig work, retiming, resampling, denoise, synthesis layers, font construction, palette/format conversion, and rejected-source removal;
- all editable inputs and runtime/delivery outputs, each present as a reviewed ordinary-Git blob or canonical Git LFS object in the exact public payload commit; URLs/release locators and local-only non-build archives cannot prove reproducibility;
- output rights holder/license, third-party notices, parent/child/pair/alias relationships, and any scope-limited benchmark permission.

“Original,” “AI-generated,” “royalty-free,” a storefront URL, or an output `All Rights Reserved` label is not input-rights evidence. A simple original C7 data row may keep the complete record inline while no media input exists and no gate pass is claimed; every media asset and every claimed gate pass requires the linked provenance record.

### Gate vector and evidence-manifest syntax

The canonical gate cell is a projection of one parsed `GATE_RECORD.tsv`: seven ordered entries such as `G1=pass; ...; G7=pass`. The exact 16-field/seven-row schema in `docs/ART_BIBLE.md` section 16.4 binds scope/profile/tier, reviewer/non-owner state, the reviewed source revision, output `NONE` at production Gates 1–4 and the current output at Gates 5–7 (the registry digest at every rollup gate), recomputed evidence/review records, build, timestamp, and defects. The only non-`pass` completion values are `STATIC_STATE_EQ`, `NON_ROM_DELIVERY_EQ`, and `INHERITED_CHILD_EQ`; `PENDING` and `fail` are non-completion states. `N/A`, `done`, a percentage, arbitrary digest containment, malformed status case such as `SOURCE`, or a path without a hash is invalid.

Every gate directory contains `EVIDENCE_MANIFEST.sha256` in the exact six-TAB-field UTF-8/LF/path/token/sort format of `docs/ART_BIBLE.md` section 16.3. The ledger records its repository-relative path and 64-lowercase-hex SHA-256. Reviewers approve that recomputed manifest hash, not a mutable folder. Unsafe/absolute/parent paths, symlinks, duplicate or case-colliding paths, malformed fields, self-membership, transitive cycles, changed/unlisted/inaccessible files, or a byte/hash mismatch invalidate the decision. If a path/hash is populated or a pass is claimed, every conditional member and the manifest hash must be retrieved and recomputed; `PENDING` alone may omit an uncreated manifest.

Canonical assets reviewed as `RIGGED_MODEL`, `STATIC_MODEL_ENV`, or `ANIMATION`, and any other canonical asset whose transitive source graph owns `.blend`, also require exact G2 and G5 `AUTHORING_STACK_RECEIPT.txt` records. The two receipts bind their gate row's source/output/build identity to the reviewed historical `config/toolchain.lock.json` and the complete versioned checker/producer bundle. They must be direct ordinary-Git members of their own gate evidence manifests at `role:authoring.stack_receipt`; rollups, unrelated profiles without Blender source, every other gate, nested-only ownership, output-manifest ownership, and multiple ownership forbid the record. Gate 5's receipt is emitted only by the deterministic export wrapper after equal passing pre/post stack checks.

Ordinary reviewed blobs must byte-equal the reviewed commit. Canonical Git LFS pointers are validated by attribute, pointer OID/size, materialized bytes, manifest count/hash, and credential-free fresh-public-clone object retrieval. All art-review evidence is Git/LFS-only: a release URL/locator file cannot support a pass. Across a manifest graph, member paths, materialized content hashes, and non-`-` capture IDs have one owner.

For an explicit row at `source` or later, the provenance cell begins `record:review/<id>/g1/PROVENANCE.md@<sha256>`, editable-source cell is exactly `source:assets-src/<prefix>/<id>/SOURCE_MANIFEST.sha256@<sha256>`, output cell is `output:NONE` only before `converted` or `output:review/<id>/g5/OUTPUT_MANIFEST.sha256@<sha256>`, evidence cell is `gate_record:review/<id>/g1/GATE_RECORD.tsv@<sha256>`, and gate-vector decisions match that parsed record. `<prefix>` is byte-for-byte the segment of `<id>` before its first dot. These machine locators precede any human note after `; `. They are compared byte-for-byte with the active whitelist registry where applicable.

Every `concept` or later row also uses exact machine projections in the three rights/history cells: `rights_basis:<canonical-token>; rights_evidence:<repo-path>@<sha256>`, `transformations_sha256:<64-lowercase-hex>`, and `output_license:<canonical-token>`. The exact rights/output token sets, evidence-kind mapping, and ordered record schemas are defined in Art Bible section 16.4. The rights record is parsed rather than merely hashed; only `project-original` may say `NO_EXTERNAL_INPUT`, while every other basis cites separate source-owned substantive license/terms bytes and an independent reviewer/time. The transformation digest is the actual parsed ordinary-Git file `assets-src/<prefix>/<id>/TRANSFORMATIONS.md`; it binds one owned input digest at exact role `source.authored`, `source.generated`, `source.recording`, or `source.data` (or the narrow no-transformable-member project-original marker), an exact counted ordered operation sequence, editor, and time. Metadata and rights files cannot serve as transformation input. Both records are payload-owned members of the asset's source manifest. These values equal parsed `PROVENANCE.md`; placeholders, one-byte files, arbitrary tokens, or vague labels such as `original`, `AI-generated`, or `royalty-free` fail.

Every ordinary `source.generated` member—including GPT-image storyboard and cutscene work—also owns exactly one canonical `generation.record` under `assets-src/<prefix>/<id>/generation/`, derived from the first 16 hex of SHA-256 over its source path. The exact ordered record binds model product/version, complete positive and negative instructions, seed/job or `NOT_EXPOSED`, selected output path/hash, creator, strict time, and human-edit summary. Generation records use `METADATA:generation` in subset export and cannot substitute for source content. Missing, extra, unowned, placeholder, or source-mismatched generation history fails.

Rights tokens are compatibility-checked, not merely allowlisted.
