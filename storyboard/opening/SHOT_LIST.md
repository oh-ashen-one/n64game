The exterior continuity lock is absolute: Solace's nose points screen-left and its rescue-orange tail points screen-right. Exactly four Severance gliders enter in Shot 06. Later shots may isolate one glider or carry others off frame, but all four remain accounted for; no fifth craft appears and none vanishes through an unshown story event.

## Shot 01 — Desert scale

- **Framing/lens:** Extreme wide establishing frame, approximately 28 mm equivalent; Solace occupies a small but unmistakable silhouette against the upper-left sky.
- **Camera height/movement:** High ridge-height camera with a slow, almost imperceptible push over the canyon basin.
- **Blocking/action:** Solace travels steadily toward screen-left, maintaining nose-left/tail-right orientation; foreground mesas provide parallax and scale.
- **Performance:** The carrier feels calm, deliberate, and small against the environment rather than militarized or hurried.
- **Lighting/color/atmosphere:** Warm dusk sun, ochre dust haze, long cobalt shadows, pale hull glint, and no Fracture color yet.
- **Transition:** Fade up from black, then cut on Solace crossing a distant mesa line into Shot 02.
- **Audio intent:** Sparse desert wind, a low engine thrum arriving under the wind, and the first two notes of the Solace research motif.
- **CRT-safe 4:3 notes:** Keep the carrier inside the inner 90% frame and separated from the bright sun; preserve the large horizon/ground value break after a 320×240 downsample.

## Shot 02 — Solace reveal

- **Framing/lens:** Wide three-quarter profile, approximately 35 mm; the pale hull, manta-like sun shade, three instrument pods, ventral deck, and rescue-orange tail are readable.
- **Camera height/movement:** Slightly below hull center with a gentle parallel track; no orbit that reverses the carrier.
- **Blocking/action:** Solace remains nose-left/tail-right while moving screen-left; the desert drifts right behind it and the instrument pods remain on the camera-facing side.
- **Performance:** The craft reads as a maintained research carrier at work, with subtle suspension sway rather than combat maneuvering.
- **Lighting/color/atmosphere:** Warm rim light on chalk ceramic, amber deck practicals, oxidized details, and cool shade beneath the sail.
- **Transition:** Match the closest instrument pod to the circular deck apparatus in Shot 03.
- **Audio intent:** Engine and rigging hum gain detail; soft instrument ticks and distant work-deck clatter enter without dialogue.
- **CRT-safe 4:3 notes:** Keep nose, orange tail, sail tips, and all three pods inside overscan-safe margins; the silhouette must remain distinct from the bright clouds.

## Shot 03 — Research deck

- **Blocking/action:** The foreground crew member records a reading, the central crew member tends the circular instrument, and the background worker checks the open desert rail; nobody blocks the aisle.
- **Performance:** Focused, practiced teamwork with restrained gestures; the crew are researchers responding to normal work, not soldiers posing for camera.
- **Lighting/color/atmosphere:** Amber practicals and sunset spill across worn ceramic, brass, teal equipment, and deep deck recesses.
- **Transition:** Cut inward on the central instrument's dark circular face to its close view in Shot 04.
- **Audio intent:** Deck machinery, pen/tool contact, quiet boots, restrained crew breaths, and the Solace motif carried by muted metal tones.
- **CRT-safe 4:3 notes:** Preserve clear silhouettes for all three crew positions at 320×240; the circular instrument is the single focal point and contains no fake text.

## Future ROM replacement contract

The accepted storyboard is a handoff for a later user-supplied video, not permission to add a codec or cinematic payload now. Future integration preserves these format-neutral logical paths:

- Video: `rom:/cinematic/opening_solace.video`
- Paired audio: `rom:/audio/cinematic/opening_solace.audio`
- Timeline/metadata: `rom:/data/cutscene/opening_solace.cut`

The future manifests select the concrete video and audio formats. Natural completion, a safe `{A}`/`{START}` skip, missing media, and decode failure all converge on the idempotent callback `opening_cinematic_finish`. Skip first stops further video/audio submission and then enters the same shutdown path as natural completion. Missing media or decode failure records telemetry and plays the approved `INSERT CUTSCENE HERE` slate before convergence.

`opening_cinematic_finish` must stop playback and audio ownership, wait for the render fence, release only opening-slot resources, clear all opening `{A}`/`{START}` input edges, set `opening_cinematic_seen = true`, and request `SCENE_NAME_ENTRY`. Repeated calls are no-ops. No exit path may leak an input edge, free a replayable resource before the fence, save a half-transition state, or bypass the same flag and scene handoff.
