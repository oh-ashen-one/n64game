# Opening Solace Color Script

The approved visual reference is [`COLOR_SCRIPT.png`](COLOR_SCRIPT.png). Its four phases carry the opening from human warmth through controlled Fracture violation into a cool, legible interface handoff. Hex values are sRGB targets from the project palette; animation and future video grading should preserve their value relationships rather than cover every frame with one tint.

## Continuity rules

- Warm dusk remains dominant through Shot 04; the first violet signal is an anomaly, not an early global grade.
- Fracture violet is a localized corruption body. Fracture magenta is restricted to thin edges and fissures; neither becomes generic purple fog.
- Cold-white core values are brief focal peaks. Avoid pure white over broad areas and preserve detail in Solace, gliders, restraint lines, and the colossus.
- Solace's pale ceramic and rescue-orange tail remain identifiable under every phase; do not recolor the hull to violet.
- The exact four gliders stay charcoal/deep-teal with narrow violet apertures from Shot 06 onward, maintaining count and identity under the crisis grade.
- Worklight amber bridges deck practicals to the emergency beacon. Relay cyan enters only in the final signal phase so the lamp-to-name-entry match cut feels intentional.
- UI navy and cobalt shade arrive through the night sky before the cut; the name-entry surface therefore feels like a continuation, not an unrelated palette reset.
- Review the sequence at full resolution and at 320×240 with 5% overscan, slight blur, and reduced saturation. The carrier, four-glider count, restraint action, colossus, beacon, and final lamp must remain readable without color alone.
